São Paulo, Brazil's dynamic financial center, is among the world's most
populous cities, with several cultural institutions and a rich architectural
tradition. Its iconic buildings range from its neo-Gothic cathedral and
the 1929 skyscraper Edificio Martinelli to modernist architect Oscar
Niemeyer's curved Edificio Copan. The colonial-style Pátio do Colégio
church marks the site where Jesuit priests founded the city in 1554.
